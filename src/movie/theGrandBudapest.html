The Grand Budapest Hotel is a zany, colorful and fascinating journey through old postcard Europe. Wes Anderson's films are really hard to describe or define.Suffice to say that he is unique in his style. This uniqueness is what makes "The Grand Budapest Hotel" worth seeing. It's an odd story where a story is told within a story--and it's filled with famous actors making lots of odd cameos. And, like Anderson's other films, it's full of odd characters, unusual dialog and brisk-paced editing. The bottom line is that all these weird factors work together to make a film that you'll probably enjoy--but, like me, you won't be exactly sure why.
